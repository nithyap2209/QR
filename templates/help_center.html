Getting Started with QR Craft

Welcome to QR Craft! This guide will help you get started with creating and managing your QR codes.

Creating Your First QR Code

  1. Sign up for an account or log in if you already have one.
  2. From your dashboard, click the "Create QR Code" button.
  3. Choose the type of QR code you want to create (URL, Email, WiFi, etc.).
  4. Fill in the required information based on your chosen type.
  5. Customize the appearance of your QR code if desired.
  6. Click "Save" to create your QR code.

Understanding QR Code Types

QR Craft supports various types of QR codes for different purposes:

  • URL: Direct users to a website or specific webpage.
  • Email: Create a pre-composed email with recipient, subject, and body.
  • Text: Display simple text messages when scanned.
  • Phone: Initiate a phone call when scanned.
  • SMS: Set up a text message to a specific number.
  • WiFi: Connect users to a WiFi network without typing the password.
  • vCard: Share contact information that can be saved to a phone.
  • Event: Share calendar events with details like time, location, and description.

Static vs. Dynamic QR Codes

Static QR Codes

  • Cannot be edited after creation
  • No tracking or analytics
  • Work without an internet connection
  • Good for simple, unchanging information

Dynamic QR Codes

  • Can be edited anytime without changing the QR code
  • Includes scan tracking and analytics
  • Requires internet connection when scanned
  • Perfect for marketing campaigns and content that changes

Account Management

Creating an Account

To use QR Craft, you'll need to create an account:

  1. Click the "Sign Up" button in the top-right corner.
  2. Enter your username, email address, and create a password.
  3. Click "Create Account" to complete registration.

Managing Your QR Codes

Your dashboard is where you can manage all your QR codes:

  • View all your QR codes in one place
  • Filter and search for specific QR codes
  • Edit existing QR codes (dynamic QR codes only)
  • Download your QR codes in different formats
  • View scan analytics for your dynamic QR codes
  • Delete QR codes you no longer need

Account Security

Tips for keeping your QR Craft account secure:

  • Use a strong, unique password
  • Never share your login credentials with others
  • Log out when using shared or public computers
  • Regularly check your account for any unauthorized activity

QR Code Types & Use Cases

QR Craft offers a variety of QR code types to suit different needs. Here's a detailed guide to each type and its optimal use cases:

URL

Link to websites, landing pages, social media profiles, or any web address.

Best for:

  • Marketing materials and advertisements
  • Business cards and promotional items
  • Product packaging to link to instruction manuals
  • Storefront displays directing to online stores

Pro Tip: Use dynamic URL QR codes for marketing campaigns so you can change the destination URL anytime without creating a new QR code.

Email

Pre-compose an email with recipient, subject, and body text that opens in the user's email app.

Best for:

  • Feedback forms and customer service
  • Business cards for easy contact
  • Rental properties for guest communication
  • Event registration and inquiries

WiFi

Connect to WiFi networks automatically without typing the password.

Best for:

  • Cafés, restaurants, and public spaces with guest WiFi
  • Hotels and vacation rentals
  • Conference centers and event venues
  • Office visitor areas

Note: WiFi QR codes work best as static codes since they don't require internet access to function. Use a dynamic code only if you frequently change your WiFi password.

vCard

Share contact information that can be directly saved to a phone's contacts.

Best for:

  • Business cards and professional networking
  • Résumés and job applications
  • Trade show and conference materials
  • Sales and customer service teams

Need help choosing the right QR code type?

If you're not sure which QR code type is best for your needs, feel free to contact our support team for personalized guidance.

For good scannability, your QR code should be at least 1 x 1 inch (2.5 x 2.5 cm) in size. The general rule is that the QR code should be about 10% of the size of the area where it will be placed. Remember to maintain a quiet zone (white border) around the QR code.

Several factors could affect QR code scannability:

  • Size too small
  • Not enough contrast between colors
  • Too much customization affecting the pattern
  • Poor lighting when scanning
  • Damaged or distorted printed QR code
